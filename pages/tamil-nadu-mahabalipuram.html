Mahabalipuram

An ancient town known for its temples and monuments built by the Pallava dynasty.

💡 A UNESCO World Heritage site full of history, stone carvings, and beaches.

Explore Locations in Mahabalipuram

Arjuna's Penance

A gigantic open-air rock relief carved on two monolithic rock boulders.

💡 One of the largest open-air rock reliefs in the world.

Depicts scenes from the Hindu epic Mahabharata.

View on Map

Pancha Rathas

A monument complex known as the Five Chariots, carved out of a single rock.

💡 Not actually chariots, they are carved monoliths dedicated to the Pandavas.

An incredible showcase of ancient rock-cut architecture.

View on Map

Shore Temple

A gorgeous temple overlooking the shore of the Bay of Bengal.

💡 Particularly stunning at sunrise or sunset.

Built in the 8th century, it is one of the oldest structural stone temples of South India.
