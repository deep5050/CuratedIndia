Sundarbans National Park

The largest mangrove forest in the world, the Sundarbans is formed by the confluence of the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers. It's uniquely famous for its swimming Bengal tigers.

💡 Boat safaris through the deep, mysterious mangrove creeks are the only way to explore!
